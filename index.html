Welcome

TDK Research & Consulting is a translational biomedical research group founded by Dr. Thomas Kazmirchuk. We develop artificial-intelligence–designed peptides to diagnose and correct dysfunctional blood clotting in rare and acquired bleeding disorders.

Our work integrates computational protein design, platelet biology, and clinical hematology to create precision tools that target disease-causing protein–protein interactions that conventional drugs cannot reach.

Our flagship program focuses on platelet-type von Willebrand disease (PT-VWD), a paradoxical bleeding disorder driven by gain-of-function mutations in the platelet receptor GPIbα. Using our AI-guided discovery platform (InSiPS), we design molecular tools that selectively disrupt pathological platelet–VWF interactions while preserving normal hemostasis.

Research Program

Blood clotting is governed by a network of protein–protein interactions that must be precisely balanced: too little causes bleeding, too much causes thrombosis. Many bleeding disorders arise not from missing proteins, but from misregulated molecular binding events that current therapies cannot selectively correct.

Our research centers on the molecular interface between platelets and von Willebrand factor (VWF), with a focus on how gain-of-function mutations in platelet glycoprotein Ibα (GPIbα) distort clot architecture and stability. In platelet-type von Willebrand disease (PT-VWD), enhanced platelet–VWF binding paradoxically produces fragile clots that disintegrate under shear.

By combining structural biology, biophysics, and artificial-intelligence–guided peptide design, TDK Research develops tools that directly interrogate and modulate these pathological interfaces, establishing a new paradigm for precision treatment of bleeding disorders.

In-Silico Protein Synthesizer (InSiPS)

InSiPS is an AI-guided peptide discovery platform developed at TDK Research & Consulting to generate short, highly specific peptides that bind pathological protein interfaces with diagnostic and therapeutic precision.

  • Targets flat and extended protein surfaces
  • Disrupts disease-specific binding events
  • Preserves normal physiological interactions

In PT-VWD and related disorders, InSiPS has produced peptide candidates that selectively recognize mutant platelet–VWF complexes, enabling both mechanistic insight and translational applications.
